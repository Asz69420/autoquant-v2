But it is worth being honest about where the math has assumptions baked in. The $80,000 baseline assumes you are currently paying market rate for either a SAS stack or operation staffing and that your five core workflows map cleanly onto what OpenClaw can automate. If your workflows are highly specialized, require regulatory compliance logging, or depend on integrations that are not in OpenClaw's native skill library, the setup complexity increases significantly and the zerocost assumption breaks down. The zero recurring cost claim also assumes you are running a local LLM via Olama. If you're routing through Claude or GPT API for higher quality reasoning on complex documents, you are paying token costs that scale with usage volume. For high volume workflows processing hundreds of invoices or thousands of emails monthly, those token costs can meaningfully reduce the cost delta. The honest small business ROI calculation requires you to model your specific workflow volumes against the API costs of your target LLM before committing to the zerocost framing. None of this invalidates the core argument. It just means the $80,000 is a ceiling on ROI for the ideal use case, not a guaranteed outcome for every small business that installs Open Claw.
