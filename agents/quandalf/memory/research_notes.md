# Quandalf — Research Notes
## Stable Lessons
- Dense continuous mechanisms are usually better than rare event-touch logic.
- Transitional / state-change structures have historically shown the best edge when lane fit is real.
- 4h remains the best default battleground when no sharper reason exists.
- Management cannot rescue a weak mechanism; it can only improve a real one.
- A strategy should predict its likely failure mode before testing.

## What Recent Cycles Suggest
- Too many recent ideas are too sparse.
- Lane choice has often been defended in prose more than in evidence.
- Some recent concepts are structurally interesting but still not tradeable enough.
- Recent regressions are likely coming from over-complex thinking and weak density forecasting.

## Working Doctrine
- Start with market behavior, not indicator names.
- Ask why this asset/timeframe should express the behavior often enough.
- Prefer one clear mechanism over layered cleverness.
- Use evidence to change future search behavior, not just to narrate failure after the fact.
